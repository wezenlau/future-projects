We are students of UTC Reading, developing a solution to Loruko Primary School in Karare, Kenya. We felt that it is our humanitarian duty to help these young souls, and your donation makes a huge difference for us. Partnered with the Amuka Foundation, we have never been presented with a more tremendous opportunity to express our good will.

Our Mission

The central aim of our project is to provide students of Loruko Primary School with an adequate environment for learning, where the children can thrive in the success of their academic acheivements. Unfortunately, many sanitary and health issues riddle the school, rendering children to have no choice but to suffer with diseases such as jiggers caused by parasites living in the concrete floors of the schools, as some students do not have suitable footwear.

Another problem is that African Elephants in the local area can easily detect water and destroy the existing water tanks, leaving the children with little to no water. Elephants are intelligent and get used to most attempts of deterrents used by local farmers, such as fires, loud noises and traps that injure them. Therefore, we needed a cost-efficient and effective way of deterring elephants, in a manner that does not harm the elephants in the process, as many sub-species are critically endangered.

Chilli Fencing

We needed to find an effective, cost-efficient and safe method to prevent the elephants from entering the school that also does not harm the elephants. Luckily, a technique known as chilli fencing suits all of our criteria. Chilli fencing is a popular technique used by farmers from Kenya to Tanzania, therefore there is plenty of local experience, though many do not know how it works, so we plan to educate the locals and their children on the intricacies of this technique.

Our plan involves sisal string, chillies and waste engine oil. All of these materials are very easy to obtain for repairing fences, so it is eminently practical.

Children can also learn to grow chillies as an extra-curricular activity. Natural Repellents Oils

Our team have decided to repel this flea through natural repellents such as rosemary and lavender oils.

This oil, diluted with ethanol, can be sprayed around the room using simple spray bottles, which can ward off the pests easily. We plan to educate the local people on how to make this oil and to source the raw materials from Nairobi. We will also attempt to grow these plants within the local area to reduce carbon emmissions.
