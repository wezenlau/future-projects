We are students of UTC Reading, developing a solution to Loruko Primary School in Karare, Kenya. We are extremely passionate about aiding these children who are living in rural conditions, where water and basic school supplies are still a dream. We felt that it is our humanitarian duty to help these young souls, and your donation makes a huge difference for us. Partnered with the Amuka Foundation, we have never been presented with a more tremendous opportunity to express our good will.

Our Mission

Our top priority was to improve the water supply to the school, as water is one of the most important aspects of human life. Unfortunately, African Elephants in the local area can easily detect water and destroy the existing tanks. We needed to find an effective, cost-efficient and safe method to prevent the elephants from entering the school that also does not harm the elephants. Luckily, a technique known as chilli fencing suits all of our criteria.

Chilli Fencing

Chilli fencing is a popular technique used by farmers from Kenya to Tanzania, therefore there is plenty of local experience, though many do not know how it works, so we plan to educate the locals and their children on the intricacies of this technique.

Our plan involves sisal string, chillies and waste engine oil. All of these materials are very easy to obtain for repairing fences, so it is eminently practical.

Children can also learn to grow chillies as an extra-curricular activity. Chilli-growing is a therapeutic activity and will benefit both the children's mental and physical health.​ Locals are also able harvest the chillis and sell them, as an extra source of income, or incorporate them into their cuisine. Many Kenyan dishes are enhanced with spices, such as chillies.​ Furthermore, this chilli fencing technique can be implemented in local towns or villages if the school project is successful.

We are going to cycle, run and travel the equivalent distance without using any electrical or powered means to raise funds for our project.
